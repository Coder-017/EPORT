IB MYP Visual Arts ePortfolio

An IB MYP Visual Arts ePortfolio is a structured written record of artistic growth. It documents how ideas are researched, interpreted, developed, and evaluated through visual language. The portfolio is not only a summary of finished outcomes; it is a critical account of learning decisions, conceptual development, technical refinement, and reflective judgment across the creative process.

The portfolio aligns with the four MYP Arts criteria. Criterion A demonstrates command of artistic vocabulary, visual analysis, and contextual awareness. Criterion B explains growth in technique, media handling, and material control. Criterion C documents concept building, experimentation, and compositional decision-making. Criterion D evaluates final outcomes through evidence-based reflection and clear connection to artistic goals.

Task 1 — Criterion A: Knowing and Understanding

Artistic Terminology, Elements, and Principles

Criterion A requires precise use of visual arts terminology and conceptual understanding of how formal elements create meaning. Line may define contour, direction, tension, or rhythm. Form and shape establish structure and spatial presence. Colour communicates atmosphere through hue, value, temperature, and saturation relationships. Texture influences tactile and visual perception, while tone controls depth and emphasis through light and shadow.

Cultural and Historical Context

Visual practice is always situated in context. Artistic styles emerge through historical conditions, philosophical ideas, technological change, and cultural identity. Contextual understanding requires identifying influences with specificity: period, place, movement, and conceptual intent.

Communicating Meaning Through Visual Language

Meaning in visual art is created through relationships among elements rather than isolated features. High contrast can direct attention and suggest conflict. Repetition can create rhythm and continuity, while asymmetry may generate instability or tension. Strong analysis connects such formal decisions to emotional and conceptual impact.

Task 2 — Criterion B: Developing Skills

Technical Growth and Material Control

Criterion B documents how practical ability develops through disciplined experimentation and revision. Technical growth includes more consistent line confidence, improved tonal transitions, clearer edge control, stronger compositional planning, and greater precision in layering or blending.

Experimentation and Refinement of Method

Skill development in visual arts is iterative. Effective practice involves testing alternatives, comparing outcomes, and refining methods based on evidence.

Challenges and Improvement Over Time

Strong reflection identifies causes, evaluates corrective strategies, and explains measurable progress across stages.

Task 3 — Criterion C: Thinking Creatively

Inspiration, Concept Development, and Refinement

Criterion C focuses on how creative intention is formed and transformed. Process begins with thematic stimulus, then advances through experimentation, selection, and clarification of concept.

Compositional Decisions and Intention

Decisions about scale, focal point, negative space, directional flow, and hierarchy influence interpretation. Artistic intention should guide these choices consistently.

How Creative Choices Strengthened Outcome

Creativity is demonstrated through intentional problem-solving. Strong analysis evaluates how selected choices improved coherence, communication, and alignment between concept and execution.

Task 4 — Criterion D: Responding

Evaluation of Final Artwork

Criterion D requires critical evaluation of how effectively the final artwork fulfilled its intention. Reflection should assess technical quality, conceptual clarity, compositional coherence, and expressive impact.

Audience Interpretation and Communication

Responding includes examining how viewers may interpret the work and comparing intended meaning with perceived cues.

Future Development and Goals

Strong responses conclude with prioritized next steps linked to evidence from process and outcome.
